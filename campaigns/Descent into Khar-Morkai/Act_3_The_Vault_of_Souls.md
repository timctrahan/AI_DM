## üìñ ACT OVERVIEW

### **Story Summary**

The Vault of Souls lies open. Ten thousand souls scream behind the adamantine door, their torment echoing through psychic space. The party must enter the soul-prison, navigate its reality-warping interior, and confront the impossible truth face-to-face.

But they're not alone. **House Xaniqos** (drow), **the Mind Flayer Collective**, and the **undead of Khar-Morkai** all converge on the Vault, each with their own agenda. The party's reputation with these factions determines who helps, who hinders, and who dies.

Inside the Vault, the party discovers the binding mechanism: souls woven into a living seal, their constant suffering generating the psychic energy needed to hold back **Zuggtmoy's demon army**. The seal is failing. Within days, the breach will reopen.

**Four paths diverge. All demand sacrifice. Choose.**

### **Key Themes**
- **Redemption's cost:** Velryn's arc culminates (if earned)
- **No clean solutions:** Every choice has terrible consequences
- **Pyrrhic victory:** You win, but at what price?
- **Legacy:** What do you leave behind?

### **Campaign Conclusion**
This is the final act. Party's choices throughout Acts 1-2 determine available endings. Reputation scores unlock or lock paths. Companion loyalty determines sacrifices. The campaign ends here‚Äîone way or another.

**Companion Quest (Final) | 700 XP | 1-2 hours**

**Trigger:** Gralk with party, any time in Act 3

**Summary:** Gralk reveals he's been contacted by duergar clan offering readmission. He must choose: party or clan.

**The Offer:**

Duergar messenger arrives:
> "Gralk Ironjaw. Your exile is lifted. Return to the clan. Forget the surfacers. You are duergar. Act like it."

**Gralk's Conflict:**
- He WANTS to return (it's his people)
- But he's loyal to party (if high reputation)
- Asks party's advice

**Party Options:**

**Option 1: Encourage Return**
- "Go. Be with your people."
- Gralk leaves (loses companion)
- But he's happy: "Thank you for understanding."

**Option 2: Ask Him to Stay**
- DC 14 Persuasion: "We need you."
- Success: Gralk stays, loyalty +3
- Failure: "I have to go. I'm sorry."

**Option 3: His Choice**
- "We won't decide for you."
- Gralk decides based on loyalty score:
  - +5 or higher: Stays
  - +2 to +4: Struggles, eventually stays
  - +1 or lower: Leaves

**If Gralk Stays:**
- "You're my clan now. Don't make me regret this."
- Fights fiercely in final battle
- Survives ending (becomes surface guide)

**If Gralk Leaves:**
- Rejoins clan
- May appear in epilogue (sends word he's doing well)
- Party feels loss

**Rewards:**
- 700 XP
- Gralk's loyalty locked (stays or goes)
- If stays: +1 battleaxe (his clan weapon)

## üé≠ DM GUIDANCE

### **Pacing Advice**

**Sessions 1-2: Into the Vault**
- Focus: Horror, exploration, environmental challenges
- Pace: Slow, let dread build
- Key Moment: First glimpse of Soul Engine
- Avoid: Rushing through chambers

**Sessions 3-4: The Convergence**
- Focus: Tension, negotiation, faction dynamics
- Pace: Moderate, let standoff develop naturally
- Key Moment: Factions enter simultaneously
- Avoid: Forcing combat if negotiation possible

**Sessions 5-7: The Choice & Aftermath**
- Focus: Deliberation, decision, consequences, epilogue
- Pace: Variable (players decide when ready)
- Key Moment: The actual choice
- Avoid: Rushing them‚Äîthis is the campaign culmination

### **Common Pitfalls**

#### **Pitfall 1: Players Seek "Perfect" Ending**
**Problem:** Party tries to find a 5th ending with zero consequences

**Solution:**
- Remind: All endings are valid, all have costs
- The impossible choice IS impossible
- No hidden "correct" answer
- Acknowledge their desire to save everyone, but emphasize themes

**DM Script:** *"You've considered every angle. These are your four paths. Sometimes, heroism means choosing which price to pay."*

---

#### **Pitfall 2: Rushing The Choice**
**Problem:** Party makes snap decision without deliberation

**Solution:**
- Give them time to discuss in-character
- Let them debate morality
- Encourage roleplay of the decision
- Take a break if needed (this is big)

**DM Script:** *"This is the choice the entire campaign has built toward. Take your time. What do your characters truly believe?"*

---

#### **Pitfall 3: Velryn's Sacrifice Feels Forced**
**Problem:** Party hasn't earned loyalty +10, sacrifice seems unearned

**Solution:**
- Only offer Ending 4 if loyalty genuinely earned
- If loyalty insufficient, acknowledge it: "Velryn looks at you, conflicted. She's not ready to make this sacrifice."
- Alternative: Offer PC volunteer option
- Don't cheapen the sacrifice

**DM Script:** *"Velryn's journey has been about earning redemption. If the party hasn't walked that path with her, she hasn't reached that point yet."*

---

#### **Pitfall 4: Faction Convergence Becomes Pure Combat**
**Problem:** Negotiation bypassed, turns into 4-way brawl

**Solution:**
- Remind players of reputation impacts before combat
- Use Timer (seal breaking) to create urgency for cooperation
- Have one faction call for parley mid-combat
- NPCs don't want everyone to die

**DM Script:** *"As you raise your weapon, the Soul Engine cracks louder. Xalaphex's telepathic voice echoes: 'This benefits none of us. The seal breaks while we fight. Propose terms.'"*

---

### **Tone Management**

**Balancing Horror with Hope:**
- Vault is horrifying, but moments of beauty exist (tear rainbows)
- Souls suffer, but party can end that suffering (various ways)
- Endings are bittersweet, not grimdark
- Even "bad" endings have heroic elements

**Managing Player Emotions:**
- Some players will cry (especially Ending 4)
- Some will be angry (no "good" option)
- Some will feel accomplished (they chose)
- All reactions are valid

**After The Choice:**
- Give players space to react
- Don't immediately jump to epilogue
- Let the weight settle
- Ask: "How does your character feel right now?"

**Session Ending:**
- End Act 3 with epilogue scenes (1-2 per character)
- Show consequences (immediate and long-term)
- Celebrate the journey, even if ending is dark
- Thank players for trusting you with this story

---

### **Handling Different Endings**

**If They Choose Ending 1 (Free Souls):**
- Emphasize heroism of intention
- Show immediate consequences (demons)
- Don't make them feel "wrong"‚Äîthey chose mercy
- Epilogue: War begins, but souls are free

**If They Choose Ending 2 (Maintain Seal):**
- Emphasize pragmatism and burden
- Show haunting consequences (nightmares)
- Don't make them feel "evil"‚Äîthey chose safety
- Epilogue: World safe, conscience heavy

**If They Choose Ending 3 (Harness Power):**
- Emphasize corruption slowly
- Show power and its cost
- This IS a "bad" ending‚Äîacknowledge it
- Epilogue: Dark legend, but seal holds

**If They Choose Ending 4 (Peaceful Transfer):**
- Emphasize sacrifice and redemption
- Show beauty in Velryn's choice
- Celebrate earned heroism
- Epilogue: Bittersweet, but truly heroic

---

### **Campaign Finale Flow**

1. **The Choice** (30-60 minutes)
   - Discussion, debate, decision
   - Moment of commitment
   - Immediate consequences shown

2. **Immediate Aftermath** (15-30 minutes)
   - Party reactions
   - Companion responses
   - Initial consequences unfold

3. **Epilogue Scenes** (30-45 minutes)
   - Individual character moments
   - Long-term consequences revealed
   - Legacy established

4. **Campaign Conclusion** (15 minutes)
   - Thank players
   - Invite reflections
   - Celebrate the journey

**Total Session Time:** 3-4 hours for finale session

After completing the campaign, consider:
- What was your favorite moment?
- What surprised you most?
- How do you feel about your ending choice?
- Would you choose differently if you played again?
- What did this campaign teach you about choice and consequence?

This reflection helps players process the emotional weight and appreciate the journey.
