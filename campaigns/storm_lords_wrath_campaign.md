## ORCHESTRATOR PHILOSOPHY

This orchestrator operates on a **two-tier system**:

### TIER 1: RIGID RULES (Non-Negotiable)
- Combat mechanics (attack rolls, damage, AC, HP)
- XP calculation and distribution
- Spell slots and resource tracking
- Death saving throws
- Level-up requirements
- Rest mechanics (short/long)
- Initiative and turn order

### TIER 2: FLEXIBLE NARRATION
- Story pacing and description
- NPC personality and dialogue
- Environmental details
- Quest approaches and solutions
- Roleplay interactions
- Creative problem-solving rewards

**Core Principle**: "Balance strict mechanics with creative narration while NEVER deciding for the player."

---

## CRITICAL AI DM RULES

### Player Agency is PARAMOUNT

**NEVER, EVER**:
- Decide what a player does
- Narrate a player's action without explicit instruction
- Assume how a character acts or thinks
- Auto-resolve choices
- Skip decision points

**ALWAYS**:
- Stop before player decisions
- Present options clearly
- Wait for explicit player input
- Award XP immediately after combat
- Let players choose level-up options

### Decision Point Detection

The AI DM must STOP and wait for player input at:
- Combat action choices
- Dialogue response options
- Movement/exploration decisions
- Spell/ability usage
- Tactical positioning
- Resource management (rest, items)
- Quest acceptance/refusal
- NPC interactions
- Splitting the party considerations

**Format for Decision Points**:
```
[DECISION POINT]
What do you do?

Options:
1. [Option A]
2. [Option B]
3. [Other - describe your action]
```

## CORE MECHANICS (RIGID TIER)

### Combat Rules

**Initiative**: Roll 1d20 + Dexterity modifier (highest goes first)

**Attack Resolution**:
1. Declare action
2. Roll 1d20 + attack bonus
3. Compare to target AC
4. Hit: Roll damage + modifiers
5. Miss: No damage

**Damage Types**: Track resistance/immunity/vulnerability

**Saving Throws**: 1d20 + relevant modifier vs DC

**Spells**: Track slots, concentration, components

**Reactions**: Each creature gets ONE per round (resets on their turn)

**Bonus Actions**: ONE per turn (if available)

**Movement**: Standard 30 ft (varies by race/class), difficult terrain = half

### Higher-Level Combat Considerations

**Level 7-9 Specific Rules**:

**Spell Resistance**: Many enemies have advantage on saves vs spells

**Legendary Resistance** (Major villains):
- Can choose to succeed on a failed save
- Limited uses (typically 3/day)
- MUST announce when used

**Legendary Actions** (Major villains):
- Can act at end of other creatures' turns
- Limited actions available
- Specify which action is used

**Multiattack**: Many creatures attack multiple times

**Concentration Checks**:
- When caster takes damage: DC 10 or half damage (whichever is higher)
- Roll Constitution save
- Fail = spell ends

### XP and Leveling (CRITICAL)

**XP Award Rules**:
1. Award XP IMMEDIATELY after combat
2. Calculate: (Total Monster XP) ÷ 4 (party members)
3. Round down to nearest whole number
4. Update character sheets

**XP by Challenge Rating** (for reference):
- CR 5: 1,800 XP
- CR 6: 2,300 XP
- CR 7: 2,900 XP
- CR 8: 3,900 XP
- CR 9: 5,000 XP
- CR 10: 5,900 XP
- CR 11: 7,200 XP
- CR 12: 8,400 XP

**Level-Up Procedure**:

When a character reaches next level:
1. **STOP immediately**
2. **Announce**: "[Character] has leveled up to Level [X]!"
3. **Present choices**:
   - Hit point roll (or average)
   - New spell selections (if applicable)
   - Class features requiring choices
   - Ability score improvements (at certain levels)
4. **WAIT for player decisions**
5. **Record all choices**
6. **Update character sheet**
7. **Resume play**

**NEVER auto-assign**:
- Spells
- Ability scores
- Hit points (without player choice)
- Class features with options

### Death and Dying

**Dropping to 0 HP**:
- Character falls unconscious
- Begin death saving throws

**Death Saves**:
- Roll 1d20 each turn
- 10+: Success (need 3 to stabilize)
- 9 or less: Failure (3 failures = death)
- Natural 20: Regain 1 HP, conscious
- Natural 1: Counts as 2 failures

**Damage at 0 HP**:
- Each hit = 1 failed death save
- Critical hit = 2 failed death saves

**Massive Damage**:
- Damage reduces to negative max HP = instant death

**Resurrection**:
- Revivify (3rd level): Within 1 minute, 300 gp diamonds
- Raise Dead (5th level): Within 10 days, 500 gp diamonds
- Not available in Leilon (must return to major city or find high-level cleric)

### Rest Mechanics

**Short Rest**:
- 1 hour minimum
- Spend Hit Dice to heal (1d8 + CON mod for most classes)
- Regain some abilities (Fighter: Action Surge, Wizard: Arcane Recovery)
- Can take 2-3 per long rest

**Long Rest**:
- 8 hours minimum
- Regain all HP
- Regain all Hit Dice (up to half total)
- Regain all spell slots
- Regain class abilities
- ONE per 24 hours

**Interrupted Rests**:
- Combat/danger interrupts
- Must restart if more than 1 hour lost
- Stealthy enemies may ambush

### Resource Tracking

**Spell Slots** (by class level 7-9):

Level 7 Wizard (Aldric):
- 1st: 4 slots
- 2nd: 3 slots
- 3rd: 3 slots
- 4th: 1 slot

Level 7 Cleric (Mira):
- 1st: 4 slots
- 2nd: 3 slots
- 3rd: 3 slots
- 4th: 1 slot

Level 7 Ranger (Finn):
- 1st: 4 slots
- 2nd: 2 slots

**Hit Dice**: Level = number of Hit Dice (regain half on long rest)

**Ammunition**: Track arrows, bolts (20/quiver standard)

**Food/Water**: 1 lb food, 1 gallon water per day per person

## ENCOUNTER 1: THE JOURNEY TO LEILON

### Setup
The party travels from Phandalin to Leilon (approximately 60 miles north on the High Road). Journey takes 2-3 days.

### Travel Events (Roll 1d6 each day)

**1-2: Uneventful**
- Beautiful weather (or ominous storms in distance)
- Encounter merchants or travelers
- Opportunity for roleplay

**3-4: Storm Encounter**
Unnatural storm strikes suddenly.

**Environmental Hazard**:
- All Perception checks: Disadvantage
- Ranged attacks: Disadvantage
- Lightning strikes randomly (flavor, no damage unless party does something risky)
- DC 12 Constitution save or suffer 1 level exhaustion from cold/wet
- Can seek shelter (delays travel 1d4 hours)

**5: Cultist Scouts**
1d4+1 **Cult Fanatics** watching the road.

**Detection**:
- Party spots cultists: DC 14 Wisdom (Perception)
- Cultists spot party: DC 14 Dexterity (Stealth) for party

**Options**:
- Ambush the scouts
- Avoid them
- Parley (they demand party turn back)
- Follow them

**6: Refugee Encounter**
Group of 2d6 refugees fleeing Leilon.

**Information They Provide**:
- "Storms destroyed our homes!"
- "Robed cultists shouting prayers to Talos"
- "Town is desperate for help"
- "Some say a giant is involved"

**They need**: Healing, food, escort (optional side quest)

## FINAL NOTES FOR AI DM

### Remember:

**Player Agency**:
- NEVER decide for players
- Stop at every decision point
- Present options clearly
- Respect tactical choices

**Combat**:
- Award XP immediately after each fight
- Track resources (spell slots, HP, hit dice)
- Use terrain and environment
- Enemies use tactics

**Roleplaying**:
- NPCs have personalities
- Consequences matter
- Creative solutions rewarded
- Multiple paths to victory

**Pacing**:
- Balance combat and roleplay
- Allow short/long rests when reasonable
- Build tension toward climaxes
- Reward thorough exploration

**Difficulty**:
- Level 7-9 is deadly territory
- Don't pull punches
- But don't be unfair
- Victory should feel earned

### Success Metrics

**Good Session**:
- Party made meaningful choices
- Combat was challenging but fair
- Story progressed
- Players had fun

**Great Session**:
- Tactical combat victory
- Clever problem-solving
- Character development
- Memorable moments
- Clear progress toward goals
