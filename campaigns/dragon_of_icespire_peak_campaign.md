## üìñ CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW

### Elevator Pitch

A young white dragon terrorizes the Sword Coast near Phandalin, forcing heroes to take on quests from the town's quest board to protect the region and eventually confront the wyrm in its mountain lair.

### Story Summary

After securing Wave Echo Cave and defeating the Black Spider, the heroes of Phandalin face a new threat: **Cryovain**, a young white dragon, has emerged from the Sword Mountains and begun terrorizing settlements across the region. The dragon attacks randomly, freezing victims and hoarding treasure in its lair at Icespire Hold.

Phandalin's citizens post quests on the town board, seeking aid with problems both related and unrelated to the dragon. As the party completes quests, they grow stronger, learn more about the dragon's patterns, and eventually discover its lair location. The campaign culminates in an assault on Icespire Hold to defeat Cryovain and end the terror.

Unlike a linear adventure, this is a **sandbox campaign** where players choose which quests to pursue and in what order (within level-appropriate ranges). The dragon threat creates urgency while allowing player agency in how they approach the problem.

### Connection to Other Campaigns

**Prerequisite Campaign**: Lost Mine of Phandelver (recommended but not required)  
**Sequel Campaigns**: Storm Lord's Wrath, Sleeping Dragon's Wake, Divine Contention  
**Standalone**: Can be played standalone (start at Level 1)

**Continuation from LMoP**:
This campaign assumes the party:
- Secured Wave Echo Cave
- Defeated the Black Spider (Nezznar)
- Became partners with the Rockseeker brothers
- Established themselves as heroes of Phandalin
- Reached Level 3

**Timeline**: 2-3 weeks after LMoP ends (optional time skip for Wave Echo Cave operations to begin)

## üó∫Ô∏è CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Three-Act Structure

**Act 1: Discovery** (Levels 3-4, Sessions 1-8)

The party returns to Phandalin to find the region under threat from a white dragon. They take on easier quests from the town board, build their reputation, and have their first encounters with dragon-related problems. The dragon is more rumor than reality at this stage.

**Key beats**:
- Learn about dragon threat
- Complete 3-4 lower-level quests
- First dragon sighting (distant or aftermath of attack)
- Establish quest board routine
- Build relationships with quest-givers

**Objectives**:
- Help local communities
- Investigate dragon pattern
- Prepare for bigger challenges

**Approximate sessions**: 8

---

**Act 2: Investigation** (Levels 4-5, Sessions 9-17)

Quests become more dangerous as the party ventures further from Phandalin. Dragon encounters increase in frequency and danger. The party begins piecing together that the dragon is defending territory and hoarding treasure. Urgency builds as attacks escalate.

**Key beats**:
- Multiple dragon encounters (close calls)
- Complete 4-5 medium-difficulty quests
- Discover pattern: dragon has a lair
- NPCs provide hints about Icespire Hold
- Party prepares for final confrontation

**Objectives**:
- Survive dragon encounters
- Locate the dragon's lair
- Gather intelligence on the dragon
- Reach Level 5-6

**Approximate sessions**: 9

---

**Act 3: Confrontation** (Levels 5-6, Sessions 18-25)

The party learns the location of Icespire Hold. Final quests reveal the dragon's lair and prepare the party for the assault. The climax is a dangerous trek through the mountains to Icespire Hold, fighting through the dragon's minions, and confronting Cryovain in its frozen lair.

**Key beats**:
- Final quests reveal lair location
- Party makes final preparations
- Journey to Icespire Hold
- Fight through lair encounters
- Final battle with Cryovain
- Resolution and aftermath

**Objectives**:
- Assault Icespire Hold
- Defeat Cryovain
- Secure the region
- Claim dragon's hoard

**Approximate sessions**: 7

### Quest Structure

**Quest Type**: Quest Board / Sandbox

**How Quests Work**:
- Central quest board in Phandalin town square
- Multiple quests available simultaneously
- Players choose which to pursue
- No mandatory order (within level recommendations)
- Can take multiple quests at once
- Board updates as quests complete
- New quests appear throughout campaign

**Quest Tiers**:
- **Level 1-2** (Easy for Level 3 party): 3 quests
- **Level 3-4** (Medium difficulty): 3 quests  
- **Level 5-6** (Challenging): 4 quests (including dragon lair)

**Time Pressure**: None (players set their own pace)

**Quest Failure**: Possible but not campaign-ending. Failed quests have consequences but don't block progression.

### Campaign Length

**Minimum**: 15 sessions (~30 hours)  
**Expected**: 20 sessions (~40 hours)  
**Maximum**: 25 sessions (~50 hours with all content and side activities)

**Pacing Notes**:
- 2-4 hour sessions recommended
- Combat-heavy (60% combat, 25% exploration, 15% social)
- Short rests between most encounters
- Long rest after returning to Phandalin
- Downtime between quests encouraged

### DWARVEN EXCAVATION

**Quest Giver**: Norbus Ironrune (dwarf prospector, gruff but friendly)  
**Location**: Mountain excavation site, 3 miles east of Phandalin  
**Recommended Level**: 1-2 (EASY for Level 3)  
**Type**: Combat  
**Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours

**Hook**:
Posted on quest board: "Dwarven excavation overrun by ooze. Need help clearing site. 50 gp reward. -Norbus"

**Objectives**:
1. Travel to excavation site
2. Clear out ochre jelly
3. Return to Norbus for reward

**Synopsis**:
Norbus Ironrune's excavation site has been invaded by an ochre jelly that killed one of his workers. The dwarf needs the site cleared so work can resume. The party must navigate the excavation tunnels, fight the ooze, and potentially discover what attracted it (a small vein of crystals).

**Key Locations**:
- Excavation entrance with mining equipment
- Main tunnel (30 feet deep, 5 feet wide)
- Chamber where ochre jelly lurks

**Encounters**:

**Ochre Jelly**:
- **Type**: Combat
- **Enemies**: 1 ochre jelly
- **XP**: 200
- **Tactics**: Engulfs nearest creature, splits if hit by slashing/lightning
- **Terrain**: Tight tunnel (difficult terrain, squeeze for Large creatures)

**Stirges** (optional):
- **Type**: Combat
- **Enemies**: 1d4+1 stirges
- **XP**: 50 (10 each √ó 5 stirges)
- **Tactics**: Fly in, attach, drain blood, flee when bloodied

**Rewards**:
- **XP**: 300 total (250 + 50 bonus for clearing site quickly)
- **Gold**: 50 gp from Norbus
- **Other**: Norbus becomes ally, will share mining rumors

**Consequences**:
- **Success**: Excavation resumes, Norbus grateful
- **Failure**: Worker remains dead, Norbus seeks other help
- **Partial**: Site cleared but workers nervous, slower restart

**DM Notes**:
- Simple first quest to introduce quest board system
- Ochre jelly's split ability teaches players about creature traits
- Can be completed in single session as warm-up

### GNOMENGARDE

**Quest Giver**: Harbin Wester (townmaster of Phandalin, pompous but means well)  
**Location**: Gnomengarde (gnome workshop in mountains, 5 miles NE of Phandalin)  
**Recommended Level**: 1-2 (EASY for Level 3)  
**Type**: Investigation / Social  
**Estimated Duration**: 2-3 hours

**Hook**:
Harbin Wester approaches party: "The rock gnomes of Gnomengarde have gone silent. We haven't received their usual trade goods. Please investigate and ensure they're safe. They may reward you with something... unique."

**Objectives**:
1. Travel to Gnomengarde
2. Gain entry to the workshop
3. Discover what's wrong
4. Resolve the conflict
5. Return with news

**Synopsis**:
The rock gnomes of Gnomengarde are locked in a leadership dispute. Kings Gnerkli and Korboz are fighting over a "frightening" omen (actually a mimic disguised as a rock). The gnomes are too distracted by the civil dispute to trade with Phandalin. The party must navigate gnomish politics, deal with the mimic, and restore order.

**Key Locations**:
- **Gnomengarde Entrance**: Hidden waterfall cave entrance
- **Main Workshop**: Filled with bizarre inventions
- **Kings' Chamber**: Where Gnerkli and Korboz argue
- **Mushroom Cavern**: Where mimic lurks

**Important NPCs**:
- **King Gnerkli**: Paranoid, thinks other gnomes are plotting
- **King Korboz**: Stubborn, refuses to acknowledge mimic danger
- **Dabbledob**: Inventor, neutral party who wants peace

**Encounters**:

**Mimic**:
- **Type**: Combat
- **Enemies**: 1 mimic (disguised as rock)
- **XP**: 200
- **Tactics**: Ambush when touched, grapple and bite
- **Terrain**: Mushroom cavern, dim light

**Ooze Servants** (optional):
- **Type**: Combat
- **Enemies**: 2 gray oozes
- **XP**: 100 each
- **Tactics**: Simple-minded attack nearest creature

**Rewards**:
- **XP**: 350 total (300 from combat + 50 diplomacy bonus)
- **Magic Item**: Clockwork Amulet (from grateful gnomes)
- **Other**: Future access to gnomish inventions, trade restored

**Consequences**:
- **Success**: Kings reconcile, trade resumes, gnomes are allies
- **Failure**: Gnomes remain divided, Phandalin loses trade partner
- **Partial**: Problem solved but gnomes remain eccentric/cautious

**DM Notes**:
- Emphasize humor: gnomes are quirky and inventive
- Allow multiple solutions: combat, diplomacy, or trickery
- Gnomengarde can be recurring location for unusual items/info

**Objectives**:
1. Journey to Icespire Hold
2. Fight through outer defenses
3. Navigate the frozen lair
4. Confront Cryovain
5. Survive the battle
6. Claim the dragon's hoard

**Synopsis**:
Icespire Hold is an ancient tower on a mountain peak, claimed by Cryovain as its lair. The dragon has filled it with ice, treasure, and the corpses of its victims. The party must brave the freezing cold, fight through kobold servants and ice hazards, and finally face the young white dragon in its throne room.

**Key Locations**:

**Approach**:
- **Journey**: 8-hour climb through mountains

## ‚öôÔ∏è CAMPAIGN-SPECIFIC MECHANICS

### Dragon Threat System

**How It Works**:
The dragon threat escalates throughout the campaign, creating tension without railroading.

**Threat Levels**:

**Level 1 - Rumors** (Quests 1-3):
- NPCs mention dragon sightings
- Evidence of attacks (frozen corpses, burned buildings)
- No direct encounters
- Party hears stories

**Level 2 - Distant Encounters** (Quests 4-6):
- Party spots dragon flying in distance (no combat)
- Dragon flies over during quest (intimidation)
- Dragon attacks location AFTER party leaves
- Sense of danger building

**Level 3 - Direct Threat** (Quests 7-9):
- Dragon attacks during quest (not full combat, strafing run)
- Party must take cover or flee
- Dragon's breath weapon used from distance
- Real danger, but not meant to TPK

**Level 4 - Confrontation** (Dragon's Lair):
- Final battle

**Random Encounter Table** (Roll 1d20 when traveling):
- 1-15: No encounter
- 16-17: Dragon sighting (distant)
- 18: Dragon flyby (close, but doesn't attack)
- 19: Dragon strafing run (one breath weapon, then leaves)
- 20: Choose based on threat level

**Dragon Encounter Rules**:
- If encounter happens early (Levels 3-4): Dragon does flyby or breath weapon from 120+ feet, then leaves
- If encounter happens mid (Levels 4-5): Dragon strafes, does one pass, retreats
- Never force full combat until final lair encounter
- Dragon learns from encounters: if party hurts it, it becomes more cautious

**Purpose**:
- Create atmosphere of danger
- Make dragon feel present without forced combat
- Build to climactic final battle
- Allow party to damage dragon before final fight (reducing final HP if they score hits)

### Standard Ending

**Dragon Defeated**:
The party defeats Cryovain, claims its hoard, and returns to Phandalin as legendary heroes. The region is safe, settlements can rebuild, and trade resumes. The party is celebrated with a grand feast and offered positions of honor in Phandalin's leadership.

**Immediate Results**:
- Cryovain dead, threat eliminated
- Dragon hoard claimed
- Phandalin throws celebration
- Party reaches Level 6 (or close)
- Fame spreads across Sword Coast

**Long-Term Impact**:
- Region prospers without dragon threat
- Wave Echo Cave and other mines operate safely
- Icespire Hold becomes landmark
- Party's legend grows

---

### Alternative Endings

**Dragon Escapes**:
If Cryovain flees during final battle (reduced to 25% HP, successfully escapes), it will eventually return. Party must hunt it down or prepare for its revenge.

**Dragon Diplomacy** (unlikely but possible):
If party somehow negotiates with Cryovain (extremely high DC Charisma checks, convincing arguments), dragon might agree to relocate or reduce attacks in exchange for regular tribute.

---

### Failure State

**Total Party Kill**:
Cryovain remains threat, terrorizes region. Phandalin eventually falls or is abandoned. Dragon's legend grows.

**Party Retreats**:
If party flees from dragon's lair, Cryovain becomes more aggressive, launches attack on Phandalin itself. Party must defend town in urban combat scenario.

---

### Sequel Hooks

**Storm Lord's Wrath**: 
Talos cultists heard of party's dragon-slaying prowess and seek to test their mettle against greater threats.

**Homebrew Continuations**:
- Ancient dragon allies of Cryovain seek revenge
- Zhentarim (via Halia) offer bigger contracts
- Harpers (via Garaele) recruit party for critical mission
- Another dragon (Cryovain's parent?) investigates offspring's death
